Ieoh Ming Pei, a visionary architect

Ieoh Ming Pei, known as I. M. Pei, was an American architect of Chinese origin. Founder of prestigious firms such as I. M. Pei & Associates and Pei Cobb Freed & Partners. Partners, he left an indelible mark on the the world of architecture.

Biography

Born on 26 April 1917 in Canton, China. Died on 16 May 2019 in New York, USA. United States

Training

MIT (1940), Harvard University (1946)

Main achievements

Pyramid of the Louvre, National Center for Atmospheric Research, Bank of China Tower, Doha Museum of Islamic Art

See more

Distinctions

AIA Gold Medal (1979), Pritzker Prize (1983), Praemium Imperiale (1989), Presidential Medal of Freedom (1992), Royal Gold Medal for Architecture (2010)

Career path

Recruited by William Zeckendorf in 1948, Pei founded his own firm, I. M. Pei & Associates, in 1955. He designed iconic works such as the Louvre Pyramid and the Bank of China Tower. China Tower. Awarded the Pritzker Prize in 1983, considered to be the "Nobel Prize of architecture".

Youth and Training

Born in Canton, Pei moved to Shanghai in 1927, where he was influenced by the architectural architectural diversity and the gardens of Suzhou. Studies at the University of Pennsylvania and MIT, marked by his interest in modern architecture.

Personal legacy

Family: Married to Eileen Loo, father of four children. Childhood marked by the loss of his mother and complex family relationships.

Legacy and Recognition

Leaves a significant legacy with innovative innovative buildings around the around the world. Awarded numerous distinctions including the AIA Gold Medal and the Praemium Imperiale.

Retirement and Consultation

Officially retired in 1990, but continued continues to work as a consultant for his sons firm, Pei Partnership Architects.
